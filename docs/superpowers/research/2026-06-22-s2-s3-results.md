# S2 + S3 — Results on real 1-year data

**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Dataset:** `integritybg/data/integrity.sqlite` — **46,004 contracts** (≈1 year of ЦАИС ЕОП), **13,330 organizations / 15,615 ownership roles** (streamed Търговски регистър deltas), **44,701 flags**.

## S2 — the differentiator fired on a real cross-winner link

`shared_owner_cross_tenders` produced a **real, verifiable hit** even at thin ownership coverage (74/7,140 winners):

> **ХРАБРИН ИВАНОВ ИВАНЧЕВ** (manager, EGN-hash `a6e92690e5…`) links two distinct procurement-winning firms:
> - **СУБАРУ МОТОРС** (ЕИК 130859442) — won a vehicle contract
> - **Киа България** (ЕИК 204650474) — won several vehicle contracts
>
> Same manager, two winners, same sector (vehicle supply). Surfaced purely from open data via the
> hashed-person key. The СУБАРУ dossier also shows **offshore owners** (Холибел/Рамбулан Limited) and
> a **5-company group** (Киа България, ЮНИТ БРОКЕР, ЕВРОРЕНТАЛС, …).

This proves the core thesis end-to-end: **person → multiple winning firms, from CC0 data, no raw personal data.**

### Coverage is the remaining limit (as predicted)
Only 74/7,140 winners had ownership in this slice. Two compounding reasons:
1. **Recency mismatch** (S2 finding): daily deltas cover recently-*changed* firms, not arbitrary winners.
2. **Pagination bug**: `recent_resource_uuids()` caps at ~10 deltas (`?page=N` doesn't paginate the
   data.egov.bg dataset). **Known issue — fix to unlock more deltas.** But even fixed, recency means
   delta-accumulation tops out well short of full coverage → the **full-snapshot / bulk-DB agreement**
   recommendation from the S2 findings stands. With a full snapshot, links like the one above would
   surface across thousands of winners.

## S3 — Contract Integrity Index (live, calibrated)

`flags.py` → `index.py` (noisy-OR of weighted flag severities) → `integrity_scores`.

**Flags on 46k contracts:** single_bidding 31,513 · short_tender_window 8,789 · price_anomaly_cpv 4,057 ·
annex_value_inflation 180 · buyer_supplier_concentration 161 · shared_owner_cross_tenders 1.

**Calibration finding (real):** single-bidding is **68%** of Bulgarian contracts (31,513/46,004). A naive
weight made *every* single-bid "high" (25,041 high — meaningless). Recalibrated so single-bidding is a
**low-weight baseline that must be corroborated**; network/price/concentration carry the weight; **high
requires genuine multi-signal**. Result is a sane distribution:

| Level | Count |
|---|---|
| low | 26,567 |
| med | 5,447 |
| **high** | **3,240** (7%) |

**Top "high" entities** are `buyer_supplier_concentration` cases (one supplier taking a dominant share of
a buyer's spend) — exactly the pattern worth investigating.

**Entity dossier** (`dossier.py`): factual, evidence-linked ledger per company — contracts won, € total,
buyers, single-bid rate, flags, owners/managers, co-owned companies, integrity level. Demonstrated on СУБАРУ МОТОРС.
